Suicide in Suriname

Suriname, a former Dutch colony and a beautiful country in South America, is plagued by the problem of suicide. According to the WHO, Suriname has 27.8 suicides per 100,000 people, making it sixth in world ranking for country with the highest suicide rate. Research from the London School of Economics has shown that "adolescents and young adults are especially vulnerable" (https://blogs.lse.ac.uk/latamcaribbean/2017/03/15/the-silent-suicide-epidemic-in-surinames-indian-community-can-only-be-overcome-through-cultural-change/).
